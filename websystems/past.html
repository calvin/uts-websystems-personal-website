My Super Exciting Past /s

My past isn’t particularly spectacular, let’s get that out of the way off the bat.
From memory in December 1999 my family moved into a housing commission residence, living in Willmot, NSW and I lived there for the next 13 years when I was told to move out.

Early 2014 I moved next door to my then girlfriend, now wife, and went to TAFE to study IT, I couldn’t afford to attend university at the time so TAFE was the next best thing (when you could do a Diploma for $720, 2015 is when the price jumped, thanks to the conservative Liberal state government back then).

I finished my studies, getting a Diploma and Certificate 4 of IT in networking, and then entered the workforce working on the helpdesk for Zurich Financial Services Australia.

After Zurich, I moved onto Stratton Finance as a Support Engineer (fancy title for helpdesk / desktop support) where I got to cut my teeth automating tasks (even if they were my own personal ones), I didn’t really enjoy working here and I soon left.

After Stratton, I started working for Expedia as an Associate Systems Administrator supporting the corporate environment’s windows servers and any services we provided to internal end users.
Here I automated quite a number of tasks using PowerShell scripts, orchestrator runbooks, and shifted left the task of creating host header and short URL redirections to end users by developing a self-service web portal called “Go! ©”.

On November 2, 2017, I married my Wife in the Botanical gardens up at Mount Tomah and then we went on a 2-week honeymoon on Hamilton Island.

Recently, due to my automation efforts and the creation of “Go! ©” I was promoted to a Systems Administrator.
